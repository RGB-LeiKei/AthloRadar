Karate is a Japanese martial art developed in Okinawa, focusing on striking techniques (punches, kicks, elbow/knee strikes, open-handed blocks) for self-defense, rooted in physical discipline, mental focus, and character development, often summarized by the principle, "You never attack first". Its name means "empty hand," symbolizing unarmed combat, and training involves basics (kihon), forms (kata), and sparring (kumite) to build strength, agility, balance, and self-mastery.
